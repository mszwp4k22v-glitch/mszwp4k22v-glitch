Classic Sourdough Boule

Ingredients

  • 450g bread flour
  • 325g water (80°F)
  • 90g active starter
  • 9g fine sea salt
  • Rice flour for dusting

Yield: 1 loaf — serves 8–10

“Bread baking is meditation — it forces you to slow down, listen, and trust the dough.”

  1. Autolyse. Mix flour and water until no dry flour remains. Cover and rest for 30–60 minutes. This hydrates the flour and jump-starts gluten development.

  2. Add starter & salt. Sprinkle salt over the dough, add your starter, and squeeze through your fingers until fully incorporated. The dough will feel shaggy at first.

  3. Bulk fermentation. Over the next 4–6 hours, perform 4 sets of stretch & folds every 30 minutes. The dough should grow by 50–75%.

  4. Shape. Turn onto a lightly floured surface, pre-shape into a round, rest 20 minutes, then give a final tight shape.

  5. Cold proof. Place seam-side up in a floured banneton. Refrigerate overnight (8–16 hours).

  6. Bake. Preheat Dutch oven to 500°F (260°C). Score dough, bake covered 20 min, then uncovered 20–25 min until deep mahogany. Internal temp: 208–210°F.

Rosemary Focaccia

Ingredients

  • 500g all-purpose flour
  • 375g warm water
  • 7g instant yeast (1 packet)
  • 2 tsp fine salt
  • ¼ cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • Fresh rosemary sprigs
  • Flaky sea salt

Bake at 450°F — serves 12

  1. Mix dough. Combine flour, water, yeast, and salt. Mix until a shaggy dough forms. Drizzle with half the olive oil.

  2. First rise. Cover and let rise at room temperature for 1–2 hours until doubled, or refrigerate overnight for better flavour.

  3. Pan & dimple. Pour remaining oil into a 9×13″ pan. Transfer dough, stretch to fit, and dimple aggressively with your fingers.

  4. Top & bake. Press rosemary sprigs in, scatter flaky salt, drizzle more oil. Bake at 450°F for 20–25 min until golden.
