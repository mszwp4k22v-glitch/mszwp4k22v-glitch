Baking Tips & Techniques

The difference between a good bake & a great one is knowing the why. These tips will make you a more intuitive, confident baker.

Start Here

Baking Fundamentals

Master these basics and every recipe gets easier.

⚖

Measure by Weight

1 cup of flour can weigh anywhere from 120g to 160g depending on how you scoop. A kitchen scale eliminates all variation. Most professional recipes are written in grams.

🌡

Oven Temperature Matters

Most home ovens are off by 25–50°F. A $10 oven thermometer is the most valuable tool you can own. Always preheat fully — at least 20–30 minutes before baking.

✅

Read the Whole Recipe First

Before you start, read start-to-finish. Many bakes require room-temperature butter (which takes ~1 hour), overnight rests, or equipment you might not have.

📶

Don’t Open the Oven

Every time you open the oven, you drop the temperature by 25–50°F. Cakes can collapse, soufflés will fall. Use the oven light and wait.

❄

Cold Fat = Flaky Pastry

For pie dough, croissants, and scones, keep your butter ice cold. The steam from cold butter pockets creates those characteristic flaky layers. Chill your bowl too.

🥤

Understand Gluten

Gluten forms when flour & water are mixed and worked. More gluten = chewier bread. Less gluten = tender cake. Over-mixing muffin batter makes them tough — stop when just combined.

Ingredient Science

Know Your Ingredients

🥤 Flour Types Explained

All-purpose flour (10–12% protein) is your workhorse: cakes, cookies, quick breads.

Bread flour (12–14% protein) has more gluten. Use it for chewy breads and pizza.

Cake flour (7–9% protein) is finely milled. Use when you want a delicate, tender crumb.

Whole wheat adds flavour and nutrition but absorbs more liquid — substitute max 50% for best results.
